When you can’t eat eggs or have opted to give them up, you become more aware of how frequently they’re used in both baking and cooking.

From cookies to cakes to scrambles and more, eggs provide a unique flavor and binding / rising properties that can be tricky to replicate.

But after years of experimenting with vegan and egg-free cooking, we’ve learned which egg substitutes are best to use and when. Read on for our top tips for egg-free baking and cooking!

## Egg Substitutes For Baking

#### Flax Egg

A flax egg is made by combining ground flax seeds (also called flaxseed meal) with water and letting the mixture rest for 5 minutes to thicken to an egg-like consistency.

*1 Flax Egg =**1 Tablespoon Flaxseed Meal + 2 ½ Tablespoons Water*

Flax eggs are one of our favorite egg substitutes for vegan and egg-free baking because they are simple to make, versatile, and add extra omega-3s and fiber. They are excellent at adding moisture and a slight binding quality as well.

In many recipes, 1 flax egg can be used in place of 1 small chicken egg. However, they do not work in every recipe because flax eggs don’t bind, stiffen, or rise during baking quite like chicken eggs do. That’s why flax eggs don’t work well in egg-heavy dishes such as soufflés.

We have found that flax eggs work incredibly well in [pancakes](https://minimalistbaker.com/peanut-butter-flaxseed-pancakes/), [quick breads](https://minimalistbaker.com/1-bowl-pumpkin-bread-v-gf/), [brownies](https://minimalistbaker.com/vegan-gluten-free-peanut-butter-swirl-brownies/), [muffins](https://minimalistbaker.com/vegan-meyer-lemon-poppy-seed-muffins/), and [cookies](https://minimalistbaker.com/vegan-gluten-free-gingerbread-men/).

Check out our full flax egg guide: [How to Make + Use A Flax Egg](https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-a-flax-egg/).

#### Chia Egg

Chia eggs work similarly to flax eggs.

Simply combine whole chia seeds with water and let rest for a few minutes to produce an egg-like consistency.

*1 Chia Egg =1 Tablespoon Whole Chia Seeds + 2 ½ Tablespoons Water*

We prefer flax eggs over chia eggs, but for those with an allergy or sensitivity to flax or who are not able to find or make flaxseed meal, chia eggs are a great option!

You can use a chia egg as an egg substitute in many dishes, including [quick breads](https://minimalistbaker.com/1-bowl-nutella-banana-bread-vegan-gf/), [waffles](https://minimalistbaker.com/coconut-yogurt-waffles-vegan-gf/), [cookies](https://minimalistbaker.com/blueberry-muffin-breakfast-cookies-vegan-gf/), and more!

#### Aquafaba

Aquafaba is the brine from a can of chickpeas.

For recipes where you’re looking for more of a rising quality, aquafaba is a better option than flax or chia eggs. It provides a lighter and fluffier texture.

When whipped into **stiff or semi-stiff peaks**, the texture is similar to whipped egg whites. Made this way, it works well in recipes for [macaroons](https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-macaroons/), meringues, and our [Best Vegan Gluten-Free Waffles](https://minimalistbaker.com/the-best-vegan-gluten-free-waffles/).

It can also be added to recipes **unwhipped** as an egg-like binder, such as in these [Vegan Gluten-Free Sugar Cookies](https://minimalistbaker.com/fluffy-1-bowl-sugar-cookies-vegan-gf/).

#### Store-Bought Egg Replacers

We haven’t played around as much with store-bought egg replacers because it feels like just one more thing to have around in the pantry!

Plus, they tend to be made primarily from starches (such as tapioca and potato starch) that we already use in our [DIY Gluten-Free Flour Blend](https://minimalistbaker.com/diy-gluten-free-flour-blend/).

If you’ve had success with a store-bought egg replacer, we’d love to hear from you in the comments!

#### Special Consideration

When recreating a recipe that uses eggs to create a custard-like texture, things get more complicated! We’ve had success recreating flan with **agar agar** (a gelatinous ingredient that comes from seaweed) and crème brûlée with a thick, cashew-based mixture. See our [Easy Vegan Flan (No-Bake!)](https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-flan-no-bake/) and [Vegan Chai-Spiced Crème Brûlée](https://minimalistbaker.com/vegan-chai-spiced-creme-brulee/).

## Egg Substitutes For Cooking

We’ve found that [flax eggs](https://minimalistbaker.com/how-to-make-a-flax-egg/) work well as a binder in [vegan meatballs](https://minimalistbaker.com/vegan-sun-dried-tomato-basil-meatballs/), [fritters](https://minimalistbaker.com/turmeric-chickpea-fritters/), and even [stuffing](https://minimalistbaker.com/simple-vegan-stuffing/)! And aquafaba can be used to make [vegan mayo](https://minimalistbaker.com/easy-vegan-mayo-with-aquafaba/).

When it comes to scrambled eggs, frittatas, and omelets, though, the previously mentioned egg substitutes don’t work well. Luckily, there are suitable alternatives!
