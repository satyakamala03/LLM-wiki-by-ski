# Flax Egg

A flax egg is made by mixing ground flaxseed with water to create a binding agent for baking. This plant-based alternative is commonly used in eggless recipes, making it a popular choice for those following a vegan diet or looking for egg substitutes. 

To prepare a flax egg, combine 1 tablespoon of ground flaxseed with 2.5 tablespoons of water. Let the mixture sit for about 5-10 minutes until it thickens and becomes gelatinous, resembling the texture of a beaten egg. 

Flax eggs can be used in various baked goods, including cookies, muffins, and pancakes, providing moisture and helping to bind ingredients together. For more information on egg substitutes, see [[Egg Substitutes Comparison]].
