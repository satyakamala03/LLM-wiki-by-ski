# Flax Egg Summary

Flax eggs are a popular egg substitute in vegan baking, made from ground flaxseed and water. This simple mixture can effectively replace eggs in various recipes, providing binding properties similar to those of traditional eggs.

## How to Make a Flax Egg
To prepare a flax egg, combine:
- **1 tablespoon of flaxseed meal** (ground raw flaxseed)
- **2.5 tablespoons of water**

Mix the ingredients in a dish and let them rest for about 5 minutes until the mixture thickens. This flax egg can then be used in place of one egg in recipes. However, it is important to note that the substitution may not work perfectly in every recipe, as flax eggs do not bind and stiffen during baking in the same way that chicken eggs do.

### Applications
Flax eggs work well in various baked goods, including:
- Pancakes
- Quick breads
- Brownies
- Muffins
- Cookies

While flax eggs can be a versatile substitute, they may not perform as well in recipes requiring a significant rise, such as soufflés or certain cakes.

## Key takeaways
- Flax eggs are made from ground flaxseed and water, serving as a vegan egg substitute.
- The standard ratio is 1 tablespoon of flaxseed meal to 2.5 tablespoons of water.
- Flax eggs are effective in many baked goods but may not work in recipes that rely heavily on eggs for structure and rise.
