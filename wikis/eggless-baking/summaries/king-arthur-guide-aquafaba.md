# Aquafaba Summary

Aquafaba is the viscous liquid left over when draining canned chickpeas, and it serves as a remarkable vegan substitute for egg whites in baking. This ingredient can be whipped to create airy meringues and is essential for various vegan recipes.

## What is Aquafaba?
Aquafaba acts similarly to egg whites, allowing for the creation of egg-free pavlovas and meringues. It is recommended to use the liquid from canned chickpeas rather than homemade versions, as the latter can be less reliable.

## How to Substitute Aquafaba for Egg Whites
- **Measurement**: 2 tablespoons of aquafaba is equivalent to 1 egg white.
- **Whipping**: Aquafaba can be whipped like egg whites, but it may take longer to reach stiff peaks. Using an electric mixer is essential for best results.
- **Stabilization**: Adding cream of tartar can help stabilize the whipped aquafaba and reduce whipping time.

## Recommended Formula
A suggested starting formula for substituting aquafaba for egg whites is:
- 2 tablespoons aquafaba
- 1/8 teaspoon cream of tartar
- 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

## How to Use Aquafaba
Aquafaba can be used in various recipes, with varying degrees of success:
- **Meringues**: Creates crispy, sweet meringues similar to those made with egg whites.
- **Marshmallow Spread**: Can be whipped into a vegan marshmallow spread.
- **Pancakes**: Folding in whipped aquafaba can yield fluffier pancakes, though they may be delicate.
- **Cookies**: Results can be disappointing; aquafaba does not work well in recipes that rely heavily on egg whites, such as flourless cookies and macarons.
- **Cakes**: Aquafaba is not suitable for recipes like angel food cake, which depend on the stability of whipped egg whites.

## Key Takeaways
- Aquafaba is a versatile vegan substitute for egg whites, especially in meringues and non-baked recipes.
- It may not perform well in recipes that require significant structure from egg whites, such as certain cakes and cookies.
- Flavoring is important to mask any bean-like taste in the final product.
