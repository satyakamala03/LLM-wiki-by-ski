# Leavening

Leavening is a crucial concept in baking that refers to the process of incorporating air into a batter or dough, resulting in a light and airy texture in baked goods. In traditional baking, eggs play a significant role in this process. They work in tandem with chemical leaveners, such as baking soda and baking powder, to provide a secondary rise in baked products.

When eggs are included in a recipe, they contribute to leavening by trapping air during mixing and expanding when heated. This expansion helps to create a structure that supports the rise of the baked good. Understanding the role of leavening is essential for home bakers, especially when exploring eggless alternatives, as it informs the selection of appropriate substitutes and techniques to achieve similar results.
