# Out of Eggs? The Best Replacement Might Already Be in Your Pantry

This summary explores the effectiveness of various egg substitutes tested in common baking recipes, including pancakes, muffins, and chocolate chip cookies. The substitutes evaluated were chia seeds, ground flaxseed meal, [[Aquafaba]], refrigerated liquid egg substitutes, and a combination of additional chemical leavener, liquid, and fat.

## What Eggs Do in Baked Goods

Eggs serve multiple roles in baking, including:
- **Binding**: Helping to hold ingredients together.
- **Moistening**: Contributing moisture to the batter.
- **Leavening**: Assisting in the rise of baked goods through steam.
- **Emulsifying**: Combining ingredients for a uniform texture.
- **Browning**: Participating in Maillard reactions for color and flavor.

Due to these varied functions, no single substitute can replicate all the properties of eggs, but some can mimic specific roles effectively.

## Testing Methodology

Five popular egg substitutes were tested in recipes that typically require two eggs. Each substitute was evaluated for its impact on texture, structure, and flavor.

### Results of Each Substitute

- **Chia Seeds**: 
  - Pancakes were dense and sticky with a noticeable crunch.
  - Muffins had a good rise but were heavier.
  - Cookies were thick and chewy but had a prominent seed texture.

- **Flaxseeds**: 
  - Pancakes had a tough exterior and gritty texture.
  - Muffins rose well but were delicate.
  - Cookies performed well, with subtle flavor and good structure.

- **[[Aquafaba]]**: 
  - Pancakes spread thin and had a tough exterior.
  - Muffins were flat and gummy.
  - Cookies spread into thin disks with a soft center.

- **Refrigerated Liquid Egg Substitutes**: 
  - Pancakes were slightly gummy with a mineral aftertaste.
  - Muffins lacked height and had a stale flavor.
  - Cookies spread unevenly and had a raw-dough taste.

- **Additional Chemical Leavener, Liquid, and Fat**: 
  - Pancakes were fluffy and well-risen.
  - Muffins were light but slightly crumbly.
  - Cookies were chewy but more cake-like.

## Key Takeaways
- No single egg substitute perfectly replicates the functions of eggs in baking.
- The combination of extra baking powder, liquid, and fat produced the best results in pancakes and muffins.
- [[Flaxseeds]] emerged as the most effective substitute for cookies, providing good texture and flavor.
- [[Chia Seeds]] and [[Aquafaba]] can be less reliable, often resulting in gummy or weak structures.
