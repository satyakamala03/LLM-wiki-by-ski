# Chia Seeds

Chia seeds are a popular egg substitute in eggless baking. They are known for their ability to create a thick and gelatinous mixture when combined with liquid, making them an effective binding agent in various recipes. In fact, chia seeds form the thickest and most gelatinous mixture of any substitute tested, which can help improve the texture of baked goods.

To use chia seeds as an egg substitute, typically, one tablespoon of chia seeds is mixed with three tablespoons of water and allowed to sit for about 15 minutes until it becomes gel-like. This mixture can replace one egg in recipes, providing moisture and binding properties.

For more information on egg substitutes, see [[Egg Substitutes Comparison]].
