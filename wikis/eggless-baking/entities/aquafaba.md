# Aquafaba

Aquafaba is the viscous liquid left over when you drain a can of chickpeas. It can whip like egg whites and works as a binder in batters, making it a versatile ingredient in eggless baking. Aquafaba serves as an excellent substitute for eggs in various recipes, providing both structure and moisture. Notably, it mimics the aeration of whipped egg whites more than the binding structure of whole eggs.
