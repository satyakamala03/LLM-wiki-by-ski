# Flaxseeds

Flaxseeds are small seeds that are often used as a plant-based egg substitute in baking. When mixed with water, flaxseeds hydrate and form a gel-like consistency, which can help bind ingredients together in recipes.

Flaxseed hydrates into a looser, less cohesive gel than chia seeds, which can lead to slightly denser or more fragile textures in baked goods. This characteristic makes flaxseeds a versatile option for various eggless recipes, although the resulting texture may differ from those made with other binding agents.

For more information on how flaxseeds compare to other egg substitutes, see [[Egg Substitutes Comparison]].
