# Egg Substitutes

Egg substitutes are ingredients used in cooking and baking to replace the functions of eggs, particularly in eggless recipes. One popular plant-based alternative is the flax egg, which is made by mixing ground flaxseed with water. Flax eggs can effectively mimic the binding and moisture-retaining properties of traditional eggs in various recipes, making them a versatile option for those following a vegan or egg-free diet.

Flax eggs are particularly useful in recipes for baked goods such as cookies, muffins, and pancakes, where they help to hold ingredients together and provide structure. By incorporating flax eggs into your cooking, you can enjoy delicious dishes without the use of animal products.

For more information on how to prepare and use flax eggs, see the page on [[flax-egg]].
